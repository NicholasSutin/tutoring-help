Using Blackboard and submitting assignments

Blackboard themselves have uploaded a step-by-step guide on how to use the platform. When you have the time, please consider: https://help.blackboard.com/Learn/Student 

Log onto blackboard at https://my.ctstate.edu/ or https://ctccs.blackboard.com/ultra/courses 

Navigating Courses

On the courses tab, you can switch between “image grid” and “list view” for your courses

Click the purple star on courses so they will stay at the top, in your “favorites”

You should see some links on the gray/dark side when you click on the course. This is where your assignments, course content, labs, and other course material sections are.

This image might not look exactly like yours, that’s ok.

Usually, there will be a “gradebook” or “grades” section where you can see your score.

Your Assignments

Sometimes, instructors will divide content into different weeks. Click on the folder or assignment that makes sense.

Make sure you know what assignments you have to click, and which weeks you need work done for.

Links without an underline will not have more details, and you can’t click on them. These are usually for announcements.

When you see a link with an Underline, you can click it. This will usually have more content in it, and could be an assignment.

Sometimes, an assignment will have files attached to it, like this .pdf. Click the file link, and it will either show you the content in a new tab, or it will ask you to download the content.

Ensure you read the instructions on every assignment.

Submitting Work

Scroll down on the assignment until you see this section. Click the first link, “Create Submission” to open a small text editor that you edit right there. This is not always what you should use, because it’s different from uploading a document.

The second link, “Upload Files,” is what to use for providing files like Word Documents, .PDFs, code, images, or other content.

Uploading Files

Click “Browse Local Files”

A File Explorer window will open. Find your file (where you downloaded your work), then click “Open.”

Before You Submit the work:

There are 3 options to choose at the bottom of an assignment:

Do “Cancel” to stop the submission and exit the assignment. Your work will be lost.

Do “Save Draft” so you can look at your file before you submit it. This helps to double check you are uploading the right thing.

Click “Submit” to finish the assignment right away, if you are sure you did everything correctly already.

Checking the draft you saved (from above)

Look at the file that is attached. This is what you uploaded.

First, look on the left panel. After, click “Continue.”

After clicking “Continue,” you will be taken back to the original assignment page.

Don’t worry, your work is still there. Simply click the “Upload Files” link to check that the file is there, or the “Create Submission” link to check that what you typed is there. The file or text will show up, and then you can hit “Submit” at the bottom.
